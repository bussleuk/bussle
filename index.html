The problem

Transport is expensive, and the fleet already exists — parked.

Community groups pay a premium to move people, while capable minibuses sit unused a few streets away — still insured, still costing money.

£1,500

A single day trip for 15 students can cost up to this much — putting educational and community outings out of reach.

~1,000

Underused minibuses in Bristol alone, with tens of thousands more sitting idle across the UK.

12 months

Some minibuses go a full year without a trip — while insurance and maintenance costs run the whole time.

The alternatives don't fit

Ride-hailing caps out at eight seats, public transport rarely matches the route or the timetable, and coach hire is expensive and inflexible. Groups are left choosing between high cost and no trip at all.

The solution

One route line connecting three sides of a marketplace.

Bussle matches spare capacity with real demand — and handles the parts that usually stop sharing from happening.

Stop 01

Organisations with idle minibuses

Schools, faith groups and charities that own vehicles put their spare capacity to work — turning a standing cost into value for the community.

Stop 02

Groups needing affordable transport

Community organisations book shared, right-sized minibus journeys at significantly reduced rates for schools, charities and community groups.

Stop 03

Licensed drivers

Vetted drivers — paid or voluntary — get matched to journeys that fit, keeping vehicles moving safely and reliably.

Bussle takes care of the hard parts: Insurance Onboarding Logistics
